DAY 1 - First Day on Campus

The first day of the tour was an introduction to Eurasia University and the city of Xi’an. We were welcomed by Vanessa, the local Eurasia lecturer. She introduced us to our workshop with a brief history of Xi’an. She discussed its evolution as an ancient city that was once the capital of China during the Western Zhou, Western Han, Sui, Northern Zhou, and Tang imperial dynasties. A fascinating point mentioned in this lecture was the Feng Shui of Xi’an, and how it was chosen as the perfect location for a settlement in ancient times due to its optimal natural surroundings that would protect the city from extreme weather hazards and ensure mild temperatures year-round. This opportunity to learn about Xi’an history was as interesting as it was integral to beginning to understand the context of the project we were to produce.

After this lecture, we were introduced to the Chinese students and given time to explore the campus. The lines of shops, wide roads, numerous scooters, massive sportsfields, and rows of pine & ginkgo trees were impressive, and I can’t exaggerate the gratitude I felt to be able to experience this place as a student myself. I have been overseas before for holidays and family visits, but to experience a place as a student felt entirely different and incredibly valuable.
